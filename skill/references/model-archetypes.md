# Business Model Archetypes — Kill Zones & Benchmarks

Read this file when you've identified the founder's business model type.

---

## 1. B2B SaaS

**How it works:** Subscription software sold to businesses.

**Key benchmarks:**
- Gross margin: 70-85%
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR): >110% is great, >100% minimum
- LTV:CAC: 3:1 minimum, 5:1+ healthy
- CAC Payback: <12 months excellent, <18 months acceptable
- Churn: <1.5%/month B2B, <5%/month SMB
- Rule of 40: Revenue growth % + EBITDA margin % ≥ 40

**Kill zone questions:**
1. What's your ICP (ideal customer profile) precisely — company size, industry, budget owner?
2. Is this a workflow product (sticky) or a reporting product (replaceable)?
3. What's the expansion motion? How does ARPU grow post-sale?
4. How long is the sales cycle? What's the decision-making unit?
5. What's your annual churn assumption? What's it based on?
6. Will this be product-led, sales-led, or hybrid? At what ACV does each model break?
7. What's the integration depth? Deep integrations = high switching cost = low churn.

**Common fatal flaws:**
- Feature, not product (single-use case without expansion)
- Selling to SMBs at enterprise price points
- No bottom-up virality in a PLG world
- Built on top of one platform (platform risk)

## 2. Marketplace / Platform

**How it works:** Two-sided network connecting supply and demand. Revenue via take rate, listing fees, or subscriptions.

**Key benchmarks:**
- Take rate: 5-30% depending on category
- Gross margin after payment processing/ops: 60-75%
- GMV growth as leading indicator
- Liquidity: % of supply-side listings that transact per period
- Supply/demand ratio by geography/category

**Kill zone questions:**
1. Which side of the marketplace is harder to acquire — supply or demand?
2. What's your take rate and how did you arrive at it? Will suppliers accept it?
3. Is there a chicken-and-egg problem? How do you solve it in market 1?
4. What stops the two sides from transacting off-platform once they meet?
5. Is the repeat transaction rate high or is this a one-time match?
6. Are there network effects? Local (Uber) or global (GitHub)?
7. Who are your supply-side competitors? Can supply list on 3 marketplaces at once?

**Common fatal flaws:**
- No defensible reason to stay on platform (leakage)
- Take rate set without testing supplier willingness to pay
- Trying to launch nationally before achieving local liquidity
- No network effect — just a directory with a commission

## 3. D2C / E-commerce

**How it works:** Physical or digital product sold directly to consumers.

**Key benchmarks:**
- Gross margin on product: 50-70% (physical), 70-90% (digital)
- Contribution margin after shipping/returns/coupons: 20-35%
- Blended CAC: depends heavily on channel mix
- LTV/CAC: 3:1+ for profitable scaling
- Repeat purchase rate: category-dependent, aim for >40% within 90 days
- Return rate: <15% healthy for most categories

**Kill zone questions:**
1. What's the gross margin per unit, fully loaded (COGS + fulfillment + returns)?
2. What's your paid CAC on Meta/Google today? What does that imply for LTV?
3. Is this a one-time purchase or recurring? What drives repeat?
4. What's the brand moat? Can a manufacturer white-label this for Amazon?
5. How defensible is the category from Amazon private label?
6. What's the payback period on customer acquisition, channel by channel?
7. If your paid ads become 30% more expensive (which they will), does the model still work?

**Common fatal flaws:**
- Entire model dependent on one paid channel (Meta/Google)
- Gross margin too thin to survive CAC at scale
- No repeat purchase mechanism (subscription, loyalty)
- Commoditizable product with no brand moat

## 4. Fintech / Payments / Lending

**How it works:** Revenue via interchange, net interest margin (NIM), subscription, or transaction fees.

**Key benchmarks:**
- Net Interest Margin: 3-8% for lending (retail), 1.5-3% (institutional)
- Interchange: 1-3% of transaction value
- Default/loss rate: critical to model
- Risk-adjusted margin: gross margin adjusted for credit losses
- Regulatory capital requirements: NBFC, RBI, or equivalent

**Kill zone questions:**
1. What's the regulatory structure? Do you need an NBFC license, banking partner, or payment aggregator license?
2. How do you underwrite risk? What's your expected default rate?
3. If default rate is 2x your assumption, is the business still viable?
4. What's the unit economics per loan/transaction on a risk-adjusted basis?
5. What's your cost of funds? How does that change as you scale?
6. Are you a licensed entity or riding on a partner's license? What happens if that partner exits?
7. What's the regulatory risk — is there a specific RBI/SEBI guideline that could restrict this model?

**Common fatal flaws:**
- Underestimating credit risk and default rates
- Regulatory assumption baked in that's not confirmed
- Unit economics work only at very high scale
- Dependent on one banking/NBFC partner

## 5. API / Developer / Infrastructure

**How it works:** Usage-based or subscription pricing for infrastructure or API services.

**Key benchmarks:**
- Gross margin: 60-80% (after infrastructure costs)
- Net Dollar Retention: >120% is excellent
- Usage growth as a proxy for product stickiness
- Time to first API call (developer activation)
- Developer NPS as retention signal

**Kill zone questions:**
1. What's the unit of consumption that gets billed? Is it predictable or spiky?
2. What's the gross margin after cloud infra, bandwidth, and compute costs?
3. How long does it take a developer to get to "hello world"? (Time-to-value = activation)
4. What's the expansion motion — does usage grow naturally as a customer's business grows?
5. Who is the economic buyer — developer or their manager/CFO?
6. What's the migration cost if a customer wants to leave? (Switching cost = moat)
7. Are you building on a hyperscaler (AWS/GCP/Azure) — could they replicate this as a native service?

## 6. Consumer App / Social

**How it works:** Free users monetized via ads, subscriptions, or in-app purchases. Network effects critical.

**Key benchmarks:**
- DAU/MAU ratio: >40% is strong engagement
- D1/D7/D30 retention: benchmarks vary by category
- ARPU (ads): $2-10/year developing markets, $10-50/year US
- Subscription conversion: 2-10% of free users
- K-factor (virality coefficient): >1 means viral growth

**Kill zone questions:**
1. What's the retention curve — does it flatten or does it go to zero?
2. How do you acquire users at scale without killing unit economics?
3. What's the monetization model, and at what DAU does it become meaningful?
4. Is there a network effect? What kind — same-side, cross-side, or neither?
5. What prevents a large platform (Meta, Google) from copying this feature?
6. What's the CAC via organic vs. paid? What's the blend at scale?

## 7. B2B Services / Consulting

**How it works:** Professional services sold on retainer, project, or hourly basis.

**Key benchmarks:**
- Gross margin: 30-50% (after delivery labor costs)
- EBITDA margin at scale: 15-25%
- Utilization rate: 70-80% of billable hours
- Revenue per FTE: ₹30-80L/year depending on segment

**Kill zone questions:**
1. Is this a product-services hybrid? Can any part be productized?
2. How does this scale? Revenue is headcount-limited unless you build leverage.
3. What's the average contract value and duration?
4. What's the churn rate on retainer clients?
5. How do you prevent key-person dependency? If your top consultant leaves, what happens?
6. Can you eventually build a SaaS or platform layer on top of the service?

## 8. Hardware / Physical Product

**Key benchmarks:**
- Gross margin: 25-50% (highly dependent on BOM)
- BOM (Bill of Materials) vs. selling price: minimum 3x markup
- NRE (Non-Recurring Engineering) and tooling costs
- Channel (D2C vs. retail vs. distributor): affects margin significantly

**Kill zone questions:**
1. What's the BOM cost per unit? What's the target selling price? What's the gross margin?
2. What are the NRE and tooling costs? How many units to break even on those?
3. What's the manufacturing and fulfillment partner? What are the MOQ (minimum order quantities)?
4. Is there a recurring software/subscription layer? Hardware alone is hard to build a business on.
5. How do you handle warranty, returns, and support?
6. What does retail channel margin do to your net margin?
